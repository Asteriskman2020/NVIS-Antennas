How is 97–99% achieved?

Every component is selected for minimum loss: #10 AWG hard-drawn copper wire (37% more cross-section than #12), LMR-400 coax (50% lower loss than RG-213), a triple-core FT-240-43 balun, and a 10×10 m ground mesh with 20 radials. Total system loss is only 0.31–0.42 dB.

🛠 Construction Steps

  1. Cut wire: 2×20.0 m (#10 AWG HC Cu, 80m) + 2×10.0 m (40m), strip 3 cm at feed ends
  2. Wind balun: Stack 3×FT-240-43, 12 bifilar turns #14 AWG PTFE, mount on SO-239/N-type
  3. Feedpoint: Solder 80m + 40m elements to balun terminals, 5 cm spreader between bands
  4. Ground system: Lay 10×10 m mesh, bond 20 radials at 18° × 15 m each, connect to coax shield
  5. Erect mast: Feedpoint at 12 m apex, elements at 160° angle, guy at 4 m and 8 m
  6. Tune: Sweep 3.4–3.9 MHz and 6.9–7.4 MHz; trim ±1 cm per element tip
Element Length Formula
L (m) = 143 / f_MHz × 0.95 (velocity factor)
80m: 143 / 3.65 = 39.2 m → 2 × 20.0 m
40m: 143 / 7.15 = 20.0 m → 2 × 10.0 m
⚠
SAFETY: At 1.5 kW PEP, maintain minimum 10 m distance from antenna during transmit. Maintain 24 m clearance (2× mast height) from overhead power lines. Install a lightning arrestor at station entry. Guy the mast at 1/3 and 2/3 height. Use two or more people when raising the mast.
