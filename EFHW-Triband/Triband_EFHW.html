Why higher efficiency at higher frequencies?

Transformer core loss decreases with frequency (better μ matching), wire ohmic loss is a smaller fraction of radiation resistance, and the electrical length increase produces higher radiation resistance. The 20 m band achieves near-maximum efficiency at 96%.

No traps, no tuner

The EFHW achieves multi-band operation purely through natural harmonic resonance. Unlike trap dipoles or fan dipoles, there are no lossy traps and no need for an antenna tuner. One wire, one transformer, three bands.

🛠 Construction Steps

  1. Build transformer: Stack 2×FT-240-43, wind 14T secondary + 2T primary, house in IP65 box with SO-239
  2. Cut wire: 40.5 m (includes 1.1 m trim allowance), #14 AWG stranded copper
  3. Build CM choke: 8–10 turns RG-213 through single FT-240-43
  4. Cut counterpoise: 3.05 m wire
  5. Erect: Transformer at base (1–2 m), wire up 10 m support, then ~29 m sloping to far anchor
  6. Tune: Sweep 3.4–3.9 MHz, 6.9–7.4 MHz, 13.9–14.5 MHz with NanoVNA; trim from far end
Tuning Tips
All bands high? Trim 20 cm at a time from far end
All bands low? Wire too short; splice and extend
80m only off? Adjust counterpoise ±10 cm
Target: VSWR < 2:1 at each band centre
⚠
HIGH VOLTAGE WARNING: The wire end of an EFHW carries approximately 2,500 V at 200 W on 80m. Keep the far end well above head height. The transformer end also has elevated RF fields — maintain 3 m clearance during transmit. Install a lightning arrestor at station entry. Never erect the antenna where it could contact power lines.
