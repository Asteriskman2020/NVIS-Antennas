Why lower efficiency?

Center-loading coils introduce loss resistance (3.4 Ω per 80m coil, 1.4 Ω per 40m coil) that reduces radiation efficiency. With shortened elements, radiation resistance drops to ~25 Ω (80m) and ~35 Ω (40m), making coil losses a larger fraction. The trade-off: 60% smaller footprint at the cost of 4.5 dB on 80m.

🔬 Loading Coil Theory

Why Center Loading?
Full 80m dipole = 40 m wire
50% shortened = 20 m wire + inductors
Center-loading: best efficiency vs. size
Coil Loss Resistance
R_loss = 2πfL / Q
80m: 2π×3.65M×30µH / 200 = 3.44 Ω
40m: 2π×7.15M×8µH / 250 = 1.44 Ω

Tuner recommended on 80m

Loading coils narrow the bandwidth to only ~50 kHz on 80m (vs 180 kHz full-size). An antenna tuner is strongly recommended for full-band 80m coverage. 40m is usable without tuner (~90 kHz BW).

🛠 Construction Steps

  1. Wind coils: 80m: 35T #14 enamelled on 50mm PVC; 40m: 18T on 40mm PVC; lacquer and weatherproof
  2. Cut wire: 2×10.0 m (80m) + 2×5.0 m (40m), #14 AWG stranded; cut at midpoint for coil insert
  3. Insert coils: Solder wire ends to coil connections at center of each half-element
  4. Wind balun: 12 bifilar turns on single FT-240-43, mount on SO-239
  5. Ground system: 4×4 m mesh, 8 radials at 45° × 5 m each
  6. Erect: Feedpoint at 8 m apex, 140° angle; guy mast at 4 m if needed
  7. Tune: Sweep with NanoVNA; adjust coil turns (±1–2T) and element tips (±10 cm)
Wheeler’s Coil Formula
L (µH) = d² × N² / (18d + 40l)
d, l in inches; N = turns
80m: 1.97²×35² / (35.5+86.8) ≈ 39 µH → adjust to ~30T
40m: 1.57²×18² / (28.3+47.2) ≈ 10.6 µH → adjust to ~16T
⚠
SAFETY: At 500 W PEP, maintain minimum 5 m distance from antenna during transmit. 80m wire ends are only 4.6 m high — mark with visible flagging tape. Maintain 16 m clearance (2× mast height) from overhead power lines. Ensure loading coil weatherproofing to prevent water ingress. Install a lightning arrestor at station entry.
